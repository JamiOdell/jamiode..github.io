Project: CNS Auto Purchasing App

Click the axure logo to test drive the prototype!







Developed a prototype for people to purchase safe and affordable vehicles from a peer-to-peer service through a native mobile application. Focused on creating five interaction scenarios driven by the goals of the provided persona.

The Persona Frank is an 80 year old retired architect from Seattle. He is technically capable but would be best described as a late adopter. He uses a laptop and an iPad, but both are several years old. Frank’s wife, Janet, relies on Frank to operate the technology devices they own. Frank and Janet keep in touch with with their daughters and typically do so with the telephone, but wish they could see their family’s faces more often. Frank wishes to share photos, email and video call his daughters and grandchildren who live out of town, but he struggles with the complexity of current technology interfaces.


Objective:

While an auto purchasing is not a unique idea, many novel feartures were introduced in this app - all of those informed by in-depth, user reasearch.





Research:

To properly inform the features of this app, a range of user reserach methods were implemted such as storyboarding, scenario crafting, flow mapping and journey mapping.





Design:

Every aspect of this design was informed by the research.





Delivery:

With the research and low fidelity mockups completed, the final step was to create very high fidelity screen mockups and interaction specifications for stakeholder review. Examples seen below.
